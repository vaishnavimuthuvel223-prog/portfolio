My Journey
Scroll to begin the story
Chapter One · The Beginning

I Wanted to Heal the World

Ever since I was a child, I dreamed of becoming a doctor — of stethoscopes and steady hands, of turning care into a calling. Every school I attended, every late night of studying, was chosen to carry me one step closer to that white coat.

Chapter Two · The Climb

Every Step Came With a Cost

The path wasn't gentle. Academic pressure mounted, health faltered under the weight of ambition, and the finish line kept moving further away. Still, I kept walking — because giving up was never part of the plan.

Chapter Three · The Turning Point

76%, and a Dream Deferred

I scored 76% in my 12th boards — a result I once would have called a setback. When family circumstances meant NEET was no longer the road ahead, something in me quietly broke. The dream I had carried for years slipped through my fingers.

Chapter Four · Uncharted Waters

Adrift in an Ocean I Didn't Choose

Then, unexpectedly, I found myself enrolled in B.Tech Information Technology — a world of code and logic I never planned for. It felt like being pushed into a vast, dark ocean without knowing how to swim. Every error felt like a wave pulling me under.

Chapter Five · Learning to Sail

Two Years. Countless Failures. One Skill: Resilience.

For two years, I struggled, failed, and tried again. Late nights debugging, projects that broke a hundred times before they worked once — slowly, without noticing exactly when, I learned how to swim. Then I learned how to sail.

Chapter Six · The Discovery

The Ocean Became My Home

Somewhere between the failures and the small wins, something shifted. The ocean I once feared became the place I wanted to be. I found a genuine passion for technology — for building things that solve real problems.

Epilogue · What Comes Next

This Is Only the Beginning

The dream changed shape, but the drive never did. Today, I build, I learn, I grow — one project, one line of code at a time. My journey has only just begun, and I'm sailing forward with everything I have.

"My journey has only just begun."
